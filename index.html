About

I am a fourth year graduate student at the department of Statistics and Operations Research at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. My primary field of work is on large deviations theory, under the direction of Amarjit Budhiraja.

Before that, I obtained my undergraduate degree from the department of Mathematics of the University of Athens. Particularly influential for my decision to pursue graduate studies were my two semesters studying mathematics abroad: one at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University in Florida, and one at Technische Universität Berlin.

Research

My primary area of focus is Large Deviations theory, which pertains to the asymptotic behavior of probabilities that decay exponentially fast. An effective strategy for identifying such asymptotics involves starting with a variational formula of exponential moments of functionals of a random quantity, such as a finite-dimensional Brownian motion, an empirical measure, or an infinite-dimensional Brownian motion. Proving large deviations principles requires demonstrating two inequalities: the Laplace Upper and Lower bounds. My dissertation topic is proving Large Deviations principles for some models with asymptotically vanishing noise.

Parallel to my work in large deviations theory, I am thinking about some probabilistic and statistical aspects of stochastic processes. The first topic I considered was dimensionality reduction for data of non Euclidean nature, for which standard principal component analysis often underperforms. Consider, for example, data on a torus, i.e. vectors of angles, where PCA disregards the periodicity of the data. My work on this field was to develop an algorithm that performs dimensionality reduction on the torus. More recently and thanks to a Research Assistantship by Vladas Pipiras, I have started working on some topics in long range dependence and time series analysis. A first topic considered was the construction of confidence intervals for the variance of stationary time series data that are generated with cyclical long memory. We are currently trying to understand better the behavior of such stochastic processes.
